City: Miami

General Information: Miami has a tropical monsoon climate, characterized by hot and humid summers, short and warm winters, and a noticeable drier season in the winter. The city's climate is influenced by its sea-level elevation, coastal location, position just above the Tropic of Cancer, and proximity to the Gulf Stream.\n\nHere's a breakdown of the weather in Miami:\n\n* **Temperature:** The hottest month is July, with an average temperature of 82°F (28°C). The coldest month is January, with an average temperature of 68°F (20°C). Throughout the year, temperatures typically range from 61°F to 90°F.\n* **Rainfall:** Miami has abundant rainfall, with an average annual rainfall of 61.9 inches (1,570 mm). Most of the rain occurs from mid-May through early October. The wettest month is October, with an average of 234mm of rain.\n* **Sunshine:** July has the most daily sunshine hours, with an average of 12 hours.\n* **Humidity:** The air can feel uncomfortably humid during the summer months, from May to October.\n* **Water Temperature:** The best month to swim in the sea is August, when the average sea temperature is 86°F (30°C).\n\nIn general, Miami experiences hot and humid summers with frequent thunderstorms and heavy rain. Winters are warm and relatively dry, with occasional cold fronts that can bring cooler temperatures.
