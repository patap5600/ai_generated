About San Francisco's Climate

San Francisco has a unique microclimate characterized by mild temperatures year-round. The city experiences a Mediterranean climate with dry summers and mild, wet winters. Its weather is heavily influenced by the cool currents of the Pacific Ocean and the city's topography.

Key features of San Francisco's climate:

  • Summers are characterized by cool temperatures and fog, especially in the mornings.
  • Fall is often the warmest time of the year, known as "Indian Summer".
  • Winters are mild and rainy, with temperatures rarely dropping below freezing.
  • The city experiences significant microclimates, with temperatures varying across different neighborhoods.
