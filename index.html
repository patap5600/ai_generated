General Weather Information

The weather in New York City has a humid subtropical climate, with some areas verging on a humid continental climate. Summer (May-September/early October) is characterized by hot, humid conditions with frequent thunderstorms in the late afternoon. July is the hottest month, averaging 25°C (76°F). Winters (November-March/April) are moderately cold, with occasional snow or a mix of snow and rain. January is the coldest month, averaging 2°C (35°F). Spring brings budding flowers, light winds, and rain, with temperatures ranging from cool to very warm. The fall season is chilly and crisp, so it's wise to wear layers. The city's proximity to the Atlantic Ocean moderates temperatures, making it milder in winter and cooler in summer compared to inland areas. New York City weather can vary from day to day, and even hour to hour.
